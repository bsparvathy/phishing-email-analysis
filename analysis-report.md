Phishing Email Analysis Report

Sample Used

Fraudulent New Salary Details Phish

Source: UC Berkeley Information Security Office

Executive Summary

This phishing campaign attempts to trick users into revealing account credentials and personal information by offering information about salary increases and bonuses. The attack relies on social engineering techniques designed to create curiosity and encourage immediate action.

Phishing Indicators

1. Financial Incentive

The email offers information regarding salary changes and bonuses. Attackers frequently use financial rewards to increase user engagement.

2. Credential Theft

Recipients are directed to a webpage where they are asked to provide login credentials. This is a common phishing objective.

3. Urgency

The message encourages recipients to act quickly. Urgency is often used to reduce careful decision-making.

4. Suspicious Link

Users are directed to an external website rather than a trusted organizational platform.

5. Social Engineering

The attackers exploit employee interest in compensation updates to increase the likelihood of interaction.

6. Request for Sensitive Information

The campaign requests usernames, passwords, and other personal information.

Risk Assessment

Risk Level: High

Potential consequences include:

* Credential theft
* Unauthorized account access
* Identity theft
* Data exposure
* Further phishing attacks

Mitigation Recommendations

* Verify unexpected emails through official channels.
* Avoid clicking suspicious links.
* Check sender addresses carefully.
* Enable multi-factor authentication.
* Report suspicious messages to security teams.

Conclusion

The email demonstrates several common phishing techniques, including urgency, credential harvesting, and social engineering. Users should exercise caution when receiving unexpected messages requesting credentials or personal information.
